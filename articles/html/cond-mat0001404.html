Skyrmion Strings and Anomalous Hall Effect in Double Exchange Systems.

M.J. Calderón and L. Brey Instituto de Ciencia de Materiales de Madrid, CSIC, 28049 Cantoblanco, Madrid, Spain.
July 15, 2019
Abstract

We perform Monte Carlo simulations to obtain quantitative results for the anomalous Hall resistance, RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A}, observed in colossal magnetoresistance manganites. RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} arises from the interaction between the spin magnetization and topological defects via spin-orbit coupling. We study these defects and how they are affected by the spin-orbit coupling within the framework of the double exchange model. The obtained anomalous Hall resistance is, in sign, order of magnitude and shape, in agreement with experimental data.

Doped perovskite manganites have attracted much attention lately, since they show the so called colossal magnetoresistance[1]. These materials undergo a ferromagnetic-paramagnetic transition accompanied by a metal-insulator transition. The Double-Exchange (DE) mechanism[2] plays a major role to explain this magnetic transition. In the DE picture, the carriers moving through the lattice are strongly ferromagnetically coupled to the M⁢n𝑀𝑛italic-M italic-nMn core spins and this produces a modulation of the hopping amplitude between neighboring M⁢n𝑀𝑛italic-M italic-nMn ions.

The Hall resistivity ρHsubscript𝜌𝐻italic-rho POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-H POSTSUBSCRIPT:end\rho_{H} in ferromagnets has two contributions[3], one proportional to the magnetic field 𝐇𝐇bold-H{\bf H}, and the other to the spin magnetization 𝐌𝐌bold-M{\bf M}: ρHsubscript𝜌𝐻italic-rho POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-H POSTSUBSCRIPT:end\rho_{H}=RO⁢H+RA⁢Msubscript𝑅𝑂𝐻subscript𝑅𝐴𝑀italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-O POSTSUBSCRIPT:end italic-H ADDOP:plus italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:end italic-MR_{O}H+R_{A}M. ROsubscript𝑅𝑂italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-O POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{O} and RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} denote the ordinary (O) and the anomalous (A) Hall resistances (HR). The existence of RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} requires a coupling of orbital motion of electrons to 𝐌𝐌bold-M{\bf M}, and the AHE is usually explained in terms of skew scattering due to spin-orbit (s-o) interaction[4].

These experiments found that ROsubscript𝑅𝑂italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-O POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{O} and RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} have opposite sign, that RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} is much bigger than ROsubscript𝑅𝑂italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-O POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{O}, that RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} peaks at a T𝑇italic-TT above Tcsubscript𝑇𝑐italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-c POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT_{c}, and decreases slowly at higher T𝑇italic-TT. Recently, it was proposed[10, 11]that in M⁢n𝑀𝑛italic-M italic-nMn oxides RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} arises from the interaction of 𝐌𝐌bold-M{\bf M} with non trivial spin textures (topological charges) via s-o coupling. The number of topological charges in the three-dimensional (3D) Heisenberg model increases exponentially with T𝑇italic-TT[12] and Ye et al.[10] extrapolated these results to DE materials obtaining a rapid increase of RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} at low T𝑇italic-TT. For T>Tc𝑇subscript𝑇𝑐italic-T RELOP:greater-than italic-T POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-c POSTSUBSCRIPT:endT>T_{c}, although in 3D there is not a theory of topological defects, Ye et al. were able to estimate the overall shape of RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A}.

In this work we perform Monte Carlo simulations in order to obtain a quantitative form of RAsubscript𝑅𝐴italic-R POSTSUBSCRIPT:start italic-A POSTSUBSCRIPT:endR_{A} in DE systems. We compute the number of topological defects as a function of T𝑇italic-TT and, by introducing s-o interaction, we couple the defects orientation with 𝐌𝐌bold-M{\bf M}. In this way we obtain an AHR which has the same sign, shape and order of magnitude as found experimentally[5].

In closing, we have computed the anomalous Hall resistance in colossal magnetoresistance manganites. We have obtained, as a function of temperature, the average value of Skyrmions for the double-exchange model. By introducing a spin-orbit interaction, we obtain a coupling between the Skyrmions orientation and the magnetization which results in the appearance of an anomalous Hall resistance. The sign, order of magnitude and shape of the obtained anomalous Hall resistance are in agreement with the experimental information.
