I Introduction

The discovery that some alkali-doped C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60} compounds are superconducting [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] with a transition temperature as high as 40 K for the fulleride C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60}Cs33FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:3 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{3} under pressure [6] has triggered considerable interest in the study of fulleride doped materials at low temperatures. The electron-phonon coupling constants for C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60} have been estimated both from experiments [7] and calculations [8]. On the other hand, no calculation of this coupling exists in the literature for the case of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70}. Since the associated alkali compounds display no superconductivity, with a smooth and continuous behavior of the resistivity down to about 1 K [9], it is an important issue to compare the electron-phonon coupling constant for C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70} with those of C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60}.

In the present paper we address this problem from a theoretical point of view, computing the deformation potential of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70} and extracting the associated total electron–phonon coupling constant, λ𝜆italic-lambda\lambda. It will be concluded that the resulting value of λ𝜆italic-lambda\lambda is much smaller than in C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60}, essentially ruling out the possibility of a superconducting phase at low temperatures for doped fullerides built out of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70}.

This negative result, which can be understood in rather general, qualitative structural terms, as well discussed at the end of Sec. III, has two positive oucomes: first, it provides further evidence for the s-limit-from𝑠italic-s ADDOP:minuss-wave BCS-like mechanism at the basis of alkali-doped fullerides superconductivity. Second, it sheds light on the properties fullerenes have to exhibit to be “good” building blocks of eventual organic superconductors, indicating the direction of the highly symmetric, small fullerenes (cf. also Refs. [10, 11]), a strategy which seems to be universally valid also for other compact systems, like the atomic nucleus [12].

In the production of fullerene clusters by means of laser vaporization or arc discharge the yield of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70} can be optimized, by varying the experimental conditions [13, 14], up to a ratio of about 10% of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70} with respect to C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60}. Hence, the structural and electronic properties of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70} can be experimentally studied, and compared with those of C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60}. On the theoretical side, ab-initio methods based on the local density approximation (LDA) [16] have been applied to the study of the ground-state properties of C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70}. The ionic configuration, characterized by a D5⁢h5ℎFLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:5 italic-h FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{5h} geometry, is consistent with the NMR measurements [17], and the electronic structure agrees reasonably well with UPS data. However, ab-initio methods in the case of large clusters, such as C6060FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:60 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{60} and C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70}, are computationally demanding and may not be particularly transparent. II Electron-phonon coupling

Our calculation starts with the determination of the electronic states of fullerene-C7070FLOATSUBSCRIPT:start NUMBER:70 FLOATSUBSCRIPT:end{}_{70}, and the corresponding electron density, within DFT-LDA. We include the exchange and correlation effects according to the parametrization of Perdew and Zunger [21] of the Monte-Carlo results of Ceperley and Alder [22]. No GGA corrections are considered, since LDA is known to yield very accurate results for the equilibrium geometry and charge density in such covalently bonded carbon sistems [23]. The role of carbon atoms is taken into account using ab-initio norm-conserving pseudopotentials [24]. A spherical basis, made up with states which are solution of a central potential which mimics the L𝐿italic-LL=0 component of the LDA local potential, is employed. Spherical wavefunctions with n𝑛italic-nn and l𝑙italic-ll respectively up to 15 and 20 are included in the basis (this gives ≈RELOP:approximately-equals\approx 4000 basis vectors). The Kohn-Sham equations are solved in matrix form on this basis, as described in more detail in Ref. [25]. In that work the maximum values of n𝑛italic-nn and l𝑙italic-ll were slightly different, but the results are essentially the same.
