In the important paper [5], Moishezon proved the Chisini conjecture for branch curves of generic projections of smooth hypersurfaces in ℙ3superscriptℙ3blackboard-P POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:3 POSTSUPERSCRIPT:end{\mathbb{P}}^{3} by introducing and analyzing the braid presentations of the fundamental group of ℙ2∖Bsuperscriptℙ2𝐵blackboard-P POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:2 POSTSUPERSCRIPT:end ADDOP:set-minus italic-B{\mathbb{P}}^{2}\setminus B.

Recently this approach was substantially developed by Vik. Kulikov [4]. He proved that, for a given branch curve B𝐵italic-BB, the generic morphism is unique provided that its degree is greater than a certain function depending on the curve B𝐵italic-BB (the explicit expression is given in formula (1) below). This allowed him to prove the Chisini conjecture for a wide class of generic morphism (for instance, for pluri-canonical morphisms of surfaces of general type).

Our (rather modest) contribution is that one can estimate Kulikov’s function from above by using the Bogomolov–Miyaoka–Yau (BMY) inequalities.

In what follows we say that the Chisini conjecture holds for a class of generic morphisms if every morphism in this class is determined by its branch curve up to equivalence.
