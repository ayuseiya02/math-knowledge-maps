Abstract

The Ginzburg-Landau phase transition model is considered in d=4-œµùëë4italic-œµitalic-d RELOP:equals NUMBER:4 ADDOP:minus italic-epsilond=4-\epsilon dimensions within the renormalization group (RG) approach. The problem of existence of the non-Gaussian fixed point is discussed. An equation is derived from the first principles of the RG theory (under the assumption that the fixed point exists) for calculation of the correction-to-scaling term in the asymptotic expansion of the two-point correlation (Green‚Äôs) function. It is demonstrated clearly that, within the framework of the standard methods (well justified in the vicinity of the fixed point) used in the perturbative RG theory, this equation leads to an unremovable contradiction with the known RG results. Thus, in its very basics, the RG field theory in 4-œµ4italic-œµNUMBER:4 ADDOP:minus italic-epsilon4-\epsilon dimensions is contradictory. To avoid the contradiction, we conclude that such a non-Gaussian fixed point, as predicted by the RG field theory, does not exist. Our consideration does not exclude existence of a different fixed point.

1 Introduction

Since the famous work of K.G.¬†Wilson and M.E.¬†Fisher¬†[1] the renormalization group (RG) field theory has been widely used in calculations of critical exponents¬†[2, 3]. The basic hypothesis of this theory is the existence of a certain fixed point for the RG transformation. However, the existence of such a stable fixed point for the Ginzburg‚ÄìLandau model (which lies in the basis of the field theory) has not been proven mathematically in the case of the spatial dimensionality d<4ùëë4italic-d RELOP:less-than NUMBER:4d<4. The fact that such a fixed point can be found within a scheme of self‚Äìconsistent approximations, assuming its existence at the very beginning, cannot be regarded as a mathematical proof. An attempt to prove the existence of the non‚ÄìGaussian fixed point in 4-œµ4italic-œµNUMBER:4 ADDOP:minus italic-epsilon4-\epsilon dimensions has been made in Ref.¬†[4]. The authors have considered some rather artificial œÜ4superscriptùúë4italic-varphi POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:4 POSTSUPERSCRIPT:end\varphi^{4}‚Äìtype model, supposing that it simulates the Ginzburg‚ÄìLandau model in 4-œµ4italic-œµNUMBER:4 ADDOP:minus italic-epsilon4-\epsilon dimensions. The method of proof is to solve the problem for a finite system (of linear size Lùêøitalic-LL), considering the thermodynamic limit afterwards. However, we cannot, in principle, find the non‚ÄìGaussian fixed point at a finite Lùêøitalic-LL for a system with real interaction, and then consider L‚Üí‚àû‚Üíùêøitalic-L ARROW:to ID:infinityL\to\infty. The problem is that, due to absence of phase transition at u>0ùë¢0italic-u RELOP:greater-than NUMBER:0u>0, a stable fixed point with nonzero coupling constant uùë¢italic-uu of œÜ4superscriptùúë4italic-varphi POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start NUMBER:4 POSTSUPERSCRIPT:end\varphi^{4} interaction cannot exist in a case of finite system. All such efforts to prove the existence of the non‚ÄìGaussian fixed point, predicted in the Ginzburg‚ÄìLandau model by RG field theory, are futile, since there exists an obviously simple proof of nonexistence presented hereafter.
