Membranes are of great technological and biological as well as theoretical interest. Two main classes of membranes can be distinguished: Fluid membranes and polymerized, tethered membranes. Here, we review progress in the theoretical understanding of polymerized membranes, i.e.Â membranes with a fixed internal connectivity. We start by collecting basic physical properties, clarifying the role of bending rigidity and disorder, theoretically and experimentally as well as numerically. The tricritical behavior of membranes is discussed and shown to be quite different from that of polymers. Dynamical properties are studied in the same frame-work.
