Abstract

A graded partially ordered set is Eulerian if every interval has the same number of elements of even rank and of odd rank. Face lattices of convex polytopes are Eulerian. For Eulerian partially ordered sets, the flag vector can be encoded efficiently in the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index. The c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index of a polytope has all positive entries. An important open problem is to give the broadest natural class of Eulerian posets having nonnegative c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index. This paper completely determines which entries of the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index are nonnegative for all Eulerian posets. It also shows that there are no other lower or upper bounds on c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-coefficients (except for the coefficient of cnsuperscript𝑐𝑛italic-c POSTSUPERSCRIPT:start italic-n POSTSUPERSCRIPT:endc^{n}).

1 Introduction

In the past thirty years or more, there has been much interest in combinatorial questions about polytopes and other geometric complexes and partial orders. Of central importance is the flag vector of a partially ordered set (poset) and various combinatorial parameters derived from it. One of these parameters is the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index, defined for Eulerian posets, a class that contains face lattices of polytopes. The c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index was discovered by Fine and introduced in the literature by Bayer and Klapper ([4]). It has captured the imagination, both for what is known and for what is not known about it. It embodies in an elegant way the linear relations of flag vectors of Eulerian posets (the generalized Dehn-Sommerville relations of Bayer and Billera [1]); the number of coefficients in the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index is a Fibonacci number. It is known to be nonnegative for polytopes (see Stanley [12]), but it is not known what it counts, except in special cases (see Purtill [11]). Among polytopes, the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index is minimized by the simplices (see Billera and Ehrenborg [5]). Novik ([10]) gives lower bounds for c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-coefficients of odd-dimensional simplicial manifolds (or, more generally, Eulerian Buchsbaum complexes).

Stanley ([12]) proved the nonnegativity of the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index for “S-shellable” regular CW-spheres (including polytopes). In [13] he proposes the following as the main open problem concerning the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index: Is the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index nonnegative for all Gorenstein*FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:start MULOP:times FLOATSUPERSCRIPT:end{}^{*} posets? (These are the Cohen-Macaulay Eulerian posets.) In fact, some parts of the c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-index are nonnegative for all Eulerian posets. In this paper we determine which c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-words have nonnegative coefficients for all Eulerian posets. For all other c⁢d𝑐𝑑italic-c italic-dcd-words, we show how to construct Eulerian posets with arbitrarily large negative coefficients. The proofs grow out of the ideas of [2], which studies the cone of flag vectors of Eulerian posets.
